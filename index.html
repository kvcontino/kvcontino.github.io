Personal Summary
I live a quiet life in northern Vermont with my wife and two cats.

Professional Summary
My work spans over a decade in population health, delivery system transformation, and payment reform. In practice that means translating policy documents into defensible calculations, reverse-engineering under-documented data environments, and giving decision-makers their options when policy and operational reality don't line up.

I started as the population health analyst at a rural health planning organization in northern New York. From there I spent several years at OneCare Vermont, a CMS Innovation Center ACO intermediating over $1 billion in annual provider payments, where I became the primary analyst responsible for population health model payment calculations and the primary claims analytics resource for the finance team during a period when the organization had lost most of its analytics capacity.

My current work supports California's Medicaid transformation initiatives. I build reporting products, develop quality measures, and work directly with state leaders and subject matter experts to shape how programs use and present their data.

My focus is seeking truth from facts. I want to understand what we can learn from data and tell clear, honest, and practical stories about it.
