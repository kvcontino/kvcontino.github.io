# Grain and Pivot — Working Outline

Two Daoist concepts for analytical practice. This is a scaffold, not a draft.
Source essay: `wu-wei-analytics.html`

---

## The argument in two sentences

天理 (tiānlǐ): data has structure that pre-exists your question. Find the seams; don't impose them.
道樞 (dàoshū): every frame reveals something and forecloses something else. Hold both at once.

---

## I. 天理 — Grain

**Core move:** Cook Ding doesn't cut the ox — he finds where it's already divided.

**The analytical question this raises:** Does your data have grain, or did you put it there?

**Legibility (Scott):** Making a system measurable changes it. The scientific forest looked like order and was fragile. The natural forest looked like noise and was resilient. The grain was in the noise.

- Practical test: does the structure survive a change in how you measured it? Real grain does. Constructed grain doesn't.

**Three data types** (how cleanly the methodology applies):
- Pre-existing structure (physiological, ecological, network) — grain is real, follow it
- Partially constructed (behavior, organizations, markets) — grain is real but entangled with measurement
- Heavily constructed (KPIs, surveys, engagement scores) — grain is mostly artifact; method becomes diagnostic

**觀復 (guān fù) — watching the return:** Look for the system's natural periodicity before you set your reporting cadence. The system tells you when it's ready to be read.

**Expert judgment:** Fire commanders don't deliberate — they match to prototypes built through years of direct engagement. That's 天理 perception. It's not intuition vs. measurement. It's a different epistemic mode that measurement can't shortcut.

---

## II. 道樞 — The Pivot

**Core move:** From the pivot you can see what your frame reveals *and* what it forecloses, simultaneously. Not synthesis — holding the tension.

**Borges:** The 1:1 map is useless not because it's too detailed but because it has eliminated the position from which a map is useful. The pivot is gone.

**→ Local anchor to add:** [Watertown satellite photo equivalent — the abstract made local]

**What the pivot is not:** more metrics, better metrics, triangulation. Those are still accumulation. The pivot is a different position, not more information.

**Goodhart's Law** as loss-of-pivot: when a measure becomes a target, you've committed fully to one frame and lost sight of its complement. The measure becomes the territory.

**→ Example to add:** DoSomething.org — 1.5M views, 8 sign-ups, 0 donations. Vanity metrics vs. meaningful metrics. Clearest available example of losing the pivot.

**Who holds the pivot:** The sage in Zhuangzi — implying it's a capacity, not a procedure. You can build 觀復 into a workflow. You can't institutionalize the pivot. It has to live in the analyst.

---

## III. Where They Work Together

- Real grain appears across multiple frames. The pivot lets you see this because you're holding frames simultaneously rather than committing to one.
- Heavily constructed data: the pivot becomes diagnostic. It shows you the construction.
- Together: 天理 tells you what to follow. 道樞 tells you whether you're following it or hallucinating grain you imposed.

---

## IV. The Tension Worth Naming

**The problem:** Conventional practice is goal-first. Wu wei inverts it — observation precedes question formation.

**→ Slide sentence to use verbatim:** "Measures should be downstream from goals, but there is always a risk that goals will be warped by their own measures."

**→ Diagnostic question to add:** When is goalless wandering useful? When is it benign? When does it cause harm? (The Alice question, made practical.)

**The partial resolution:** Goals that are upstream of measurement require prolonged observation. Goals that are downstream of existing metrics produce measures that look responsive but aren't.

**Qualitative data** as the institutional form of 觀復: use it *before* the measurement regime is locked in, not after.

---

## V. What This Demands (Practical Constraints)

These are real constraints, not suggestions. Written in declarative register — keep it.

- Observation before question formation *(expensive; most orgs won't fund it)*
- Legibility assessment before measurement design *(ask what the regime will change)*
- Track the analyst's influence *(measurement choices are part of the dataset)*
- Restraint as a positive output *(null results are accurate reports, not failures)*
- Distinguish data type before choosing method *(the typology from Section I)*
